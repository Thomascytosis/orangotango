Ancestry & Heritage

Your Ancestry is whatever you would like it to be. You may use any resource for inspiration. D&D races will be used as a basis for comparison.

However, the following applies:

“Half—” ancestries are prohibited.

Weapons

Weapon proficiency is provided by class, culture, or background; usually requiring choosing which weapons to be trained in.

Small Arms

Has a d6 damage die.

Lightweight enough to dual wield.

Offhand attacks use a Bonus action.

1-Handed

Has a d8 damage die or a d10 if used two-handed.

2-Handed

Has a d12 damage die and requires the use of both hands to attack with.

2-Handed Martial Training

On your turn, when you score a critical hit with this weapon or reduce a creature to 0 hit points with this weapon, you can make one melee weapon attack as a bonus action.

Before you make a melee attack and you are proficient with this weapon you can choose to take a -5 penalty to the attack roll. If the attack hits, you add +10 to the attack's damage.

Reach

Attack of Opportunity can be made as a creature enters range.

Slashing Martial Training

Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack that deals slashing damage, you can reduce the speed of the target by 10 feet until the start of your next turn.

When you score a critical hit that deals slashing damage to a creature, you grievously wound it. Until the start of your next turn, the target has disadvantage on all attack rolls.

Bludgeoning Martial Training

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with an attack that deals bludgeoning damage, you can move it 5 feet to an unoccupied space, provided the target is no more than one size larger than you.

When you score a critical hit that deals bludgeoning damage to a creature, attack rolls against that creature are made with advantage until the start of your next turn.

Piercing Martial Training

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with an attack that deals piercing damage, you can reroll one of the attack's damage dice, and you must use the new roll.

When you score a critical hit that deals piercing damage to a creature, you can roll one additional damage die when determining the extra piercing damage the target takes.

Spellcasting

Casting spells is different for each caster depending on the source of their magic.

Charisma is the ability for Affinity; magic that is bestowed or contracted (pact). Clerics, Warlocks, and sometimes Paladins.

Wisdom is the ability for Connection; magic that manifests through awareness and intuition. Often Druids, Monks, and sometimes Rangers.

Intelligence is the ability for Wizardry; magic that manifests through meticulous understanding and calculated formula. Wizards, and sometimes Eldritch Knights, Arcane Tricksters and Rangers

Constitution is the ability for Sorcery; magic that manifests through the physical process of controlling magic as a conduit of The Source. Sorcerers, and sometimes Bards and Paladins.

Spells Known and Prepared

A spell caster can choose any spell regardless of class or school.

Many spells can be cast as rituals regardless if it has the ritual tag.

Wizardry is the only casting type that can prepare spells for the day. All other types are limited to the spells they choose to know.

Spells Known for non-wizardry users will use the Bard class spell progression. Half casters use the Ranger class spell progression.

Conjuring and Summoning

All spells that transport or fabricate creatures or objects into being or existence require a Ritual casting, minimum 1-hour per spell level casting time, and valuable spell components which are consumed.

Ritual conjured Creatures and Objects are permanent and do not disappear at the end of the spell’s duration.

The Source

Magic is the metaphysical substance of creation and existence. Magic emanates and flows through the world carving channels, springs, and clouds of raw energy. Magic is effervescent, intangible, and obsequious. Magic is Chaos, balance, and order. Magic encompasses, divides, and shifts. Magic is the Atom’s electron cloud of existence.

Cantrips

Control Flames

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: 60 feet

Components: S

Duration: Instantaneous or 1 hour (see below)

You choose a non-magical flame that you can see within range and that fits within a 5-foot cube. You affect it in one of the following ways:

You instantaneously expand the flame 5 feet in one direction, provided that a creature, wood, or other fuel is present in the new location. A creature must make a Dexterity saving throw to avoid being set on fire. On a failure, the creature must spend an action to extinguish the flame or take 1d6 fire damage at the end of the creature’s turn. This spell's damage and expanded distance increases by 1d6 and 5ft when you reach 5th level (2d6 and 10ft), 11th level (3d6 and 15ft), and 17th level (4d6 and 20ft).

You instantaneously extinguish the flames within the cube.

You double or halve the area of bright light and dim light cast by the flame, change its color, or both. The change lasts for 1 hour.

You cause simple shapes—such as the vague form of a creature, an inanimate object, or a location—to appear within the flames and animate as you like. The shapes last for 1 hour.

If you cast this spell multiple times, you can have up to three non-instantaneous effects created by it active at a time, and you can dismiss such an effect as an action.

Gust

Casting Time: 1 Action

Range: 30 feet

Components: S

Duration: Instantaneous

One Medium or smaller creature or object that you choose must succeed on a Strength saving throw and take 1d6 bludgeoning damage and/or be pushed up to 5 feet. This spell's damage and push increases by 1d6 and 5ft when you reach 5th level (2d6 and 10ft), 11th level (3d6 and 15ft), and 17th level (4d6 and 20ft).

You create a harmless sensory effect using air, such as causing leaves to rustle, wind to slam shutters closed, or your clothing to ripple in a breeze.

Mold Earth

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: 30 feet

Components: S

Duration: Instantaneous

You choose a portion of dirt or stone that you can see within range and that fits within a 5-foot cube. You manipulate it in one of the following ways:

If you target an area of loose earth and stone, you can instantaneously excavate it, move it along the ground, and deposit it up to 5 feet away. If deposited at a creature, the creature takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage on a failed Dexterity saving throw.

You cause shapes, colors, or both to appear on the dirt or stone, spelling out words, creating images, or shaping patterns.

If the dirt or stone you target is on the ground, you cause it to become difficult terrain. Alternatively, you can cause the ground to become normal terrain if it is already difficult terrain.

This spell's area of effect and deposit distance increases by 5ft, and by 1d6 damage when you reach 5th level (10ft, 2d6), 11th level (15ft, 3d6), and 17th level (20ft, 4d6).

Shape Water

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: 30 feet

Components: S

Duration: Instantaneous or 1 hour (see below)

You choose an area of water that you can see within range and that fits within a 5-foot cube. You manipulate it in one of the following ways:

You instantaneously move or otherwise change the flow of the water as you direct, up to 5 feet in any direction. If directed at a creature, the creature takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage on a failed Strength saving throw.

You cause the water to form into simple shapes and animate at your direction. This change lasts for 1 hour.

You change the water's color or opacity. The water must be changed in the same way throughout. This change lasts for 1 hour.

You freeze the water, causing submerged creatures and objects to become frozen (restrained condition) on a failed Constitution saving throw. The water unfreezes in 1 hour. A creature can make a Strength (Athletics) check against your Spell DC to break free or through.

This spell's area of effect and movement increases by 5ft and damage by 1d6 when you reach 5th level (10ft and 2d6), 11th level (15ft and 3d6), and 17th level (20ft and 4d6).

If you cast this spell multiple times, you can have no more than three of its non-instantaneous effects active at a time, and you can dismiss such an effect as an action.
